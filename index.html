About Us

GITAM Aero Astro Club is a student-run club that focuses on fostering knowledge about space and robotics. The club is segregated into two teams: - Team StarGazers and Team ROBusta. Members of Team StarGazers engage in research-led activities and conduct fun and educational events related to space, cosmos and everything in between. Members of Team ROBusta conduct workshops and work on projects to raise awareness about robotics. We are committed to making strides in the fields of astronomy and robotics and we constantly strive to achieve our goals.

  • Diana's Eve

GITAM's First Astronomy event.

The day of November 8th, 2022 was an eventful one for space enthusiasts all over as a Total Lunar Eclipse occurred that night. Dubbed as the "Beaver Blood Moon"--owing to it's reddish hue--this particular Total Lunar Eclipse was quite special as it won't occur again until 2025. It was also an eventful day for GAAC, as one of the teams, Team StarGazers organized its first-ever event that day called, "Diana's Eve". The event was a Total Lunar Eclipse viewing event and it was conducted to foster the interest in Astronomy and Cosmology amongst the students of GITAM. Students from all branches alike--from Engineering to Humanities--came to take part in the event. Members of GAAC explained about the Total Lunar Eclipse to the participants. Before the viewing itself, our members conducted a series of fun quizzes about the Moon and the participants took part actively. After the quizzes, all the attendees one by one observed the Beaver Blood Moon through a high-grade telescope. Later in the evening, the Dean of Engineering graced the event and also took part in the viewing. The event was a resounding success and also a precedent to many more fun and informative events in the future.
